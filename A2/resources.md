# A2 French Resources: Reading & Watching

A curated list of 20 resources to help you practice your French at the A2 level.

## Reading Practice

1. **[L'Arabe du Futur](https://www.google.com/search?q=L%27Arabe+du+Futur+Riad+Sattouf)** (Comic Series)  
   An autobiographical comic by Riad Sattouf using simple, everyday language and vivid illustrations.
2. **[Le Petit Nicolas](https://www.google.com/search?q=Le+Petit+Nicolas+René+Goscinny)** (Children's Stories)  
   Humorous tales about a schoolboy in 1950s France. Great for accessible vocabulary.
3. **[Astérix le Gaulois](https://www.asterix.com/)** (Comic Books)  
   Classic comics with plenty of visual context to help follow the story.
4. **[Le Petit Prince](https://www.thelittleprince.com/)** (Classic Tale)  
   Poetic but simple language; a must-read for every French learner.
5. **[Les Misérables (Adapted)](https://www.google.com/search?q=Les+Misérables+abridged+French+learners)** (Literature)  
   Simplified versions of Hugo's classic allow you to enjoy the story at an A2 level.
6. **[Carmen (Adapted)](https://www.google.com/search?q=Carmen+Prosper+Mérimée+simplified+French)** (Literature)  
   A gripping story adapted with accessible vocabulary.
7. **[La Mare au Diable (Simplified)](https://www.google.com/search?q=La+Mare+au+Diable+George+Sand+simplified)** (Literature)  
   Straightforward prose depicting rural French life in an adapted format.
8. **[Les Cahiers d'Esther](https://www.google.com/search?q=Les+Cahiers+d%27Esther+Riad+Sattouf)** (Comic Strip)  
   Daily life of a young girl, great for learning modern, authentic expressions.
9. **[Fabulang A2 Stories](https://fabulang.com/)** (Reading Practice)  
   Short, level-appropriate stories often accompanied by English translations.
10. **[Lingua.com French Texts](https://lingua.com/french/reading/)** (Educational)  
    Short texts on various topics with comprehension questions.
11. **[Kwiziq Reading Practice](https://french.kwiziq.com/learn/reading)** (Interactive)  
    Themed bilingual reading exercises (e.g., postcards, emails).
12. **[Short Stories in French for Beginners](https://www.google.com/search?q=Short+Stories+in+French+for+Beginners+Olly+Richards)** (Book)  
    Specifically designed by Olly Richards to help learners build vocabulary through stories.

## Watching & Listening Practice

13. **[Le journal en français facile (RFI)](https://francaisfacile.rfi.fr/fr/podcasts/journal-en-fran%C3%A7ais-facile/)** (News/Podcast)  
    Daily 10-minute news broadcast with transcripts using simplified language.
14. **[Learn French with Anthony](https://www.explorefrench.fr/)** (Podcast)  
    Bilingual explanations and varied discussions tailored for A2 learners.
15. **[InnerFrench Podcast](https://innerfrench.com/)** (Podcast/YouTube)  
    Slow, clear speech about French culture and society; highly recommended.
16. **[Easy French](https://www.youtube.com/@EasyFrench)** (YouTube Channel)  
    Street interviews and stories with bilingual subtitles to improve listening.
17. **[7 Jours sur la Planète](https://apprendre.tv5monde.com/fr/exercices/a2-elementaire)** (TV Show/Videos)  
    Weekly news summaries with exercises organized by level.
18. **[Les Aventures de Tintin](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Tintin+en+français)** (Animated Series)  
    Classic animated show with clear dialogue and engaging plots.
19. **[French in Action](https://www.learner.org/series/french-in-action/)** (Video Course)  
    An immersive story-based course that focuses on everyday topics.
20. **[French Mornings with Elisa](https://www.youtube.com/@FrenchMorningswithElisa)** (YouTube Channel)  
    Content designed specifically for learners to improve comprehension and pronunciation.
