# French Grammar: A2 Sentence Structures

To move beyond basic sentences, you need to master how to describe the past and connect your ideas.

## 1. The Past: Passé Composé vs. Imparfait

This is one of the most important distinctions at the A2 level.

### Passé Composé (The "Action" Tense)
Used for specific, completed actions in the past. It often answers "What happened?"
- **Structure:** [Subject] + [avoir/être] + [Past Participle]
- **Example:** *J'ai mangé une pomme.* (I ate an apple.)
- **Key Words:** *Hier, soudain, une fois, la semaine dernière.*

### Imparfait (The "Background" Tense)
Used for descriptions, habits, or ongoing states in the past. It often answers "What was it like?" or "What used to happen?"
- **Structure:** [Subject] + [Verb Stem] + [Ending (-ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient)]
- **Example:** *Quand j'étais petit, je jouais au foot.* (When I was little, I used to play soccer.)
- **Key Words:** *Souvent, d'habitude, tous les jours, à cette époque.*

---

## 2. Comparing Things (La Comparaison)

- **More than:** *plus... que* (e.g., *La voiture est plus rapide que le vélo.*)
- **Less than:** *moins... que* (e.g., *Le chat est moins grand que le chien.*)
- **As... as:** *aussi... que* (e.g., *Il est aussi intelligent que son frère.*)

---

## 3. Relative Pronouns: Qui vs. Que

These help you join two sentences into one.

- **Qui** (Subject): Refers to the person or thing doing the action.
  - *L'homme **qui** parle est français.* (The man **who** is speaking is French.)
- **Que/Qu'** (Direct Object): Refers to the person or thing receiving the action.
  - *Le livre **que** je lis est intéressant.* (The book **that** I am reading is interesting.)

---

## 4. Talking about the Future: Futur Simple

While *Futur Proche* (aller + infinitive) is used for the immediate future, *Futur Simple* is used for predictions or distant plans.

- **Structure:** [Infinitif] + [Ending (-ai, -as, -a, -ons, -ez, -ont)]
- **Example:** *L'année prochaine, j'irai en France.* (Next year, I will go to France.)
- **Tip:** Many common verbs have irregular stems (e.g., *être* -> *ser-*, *avoir* -> *aur-*, *faire* -> *fer-*).
